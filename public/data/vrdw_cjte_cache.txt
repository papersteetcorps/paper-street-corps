Scoring logic:

Step 1 — For each answer, ignore tone, self-idealization, emotional intensity, or intellectual complexity. Extract only cognitive process indicators. Identify whether the person is focusing on:-
• Concrete present reality or abstract possibility
• Logical structure or value judgment
• Internal reference point or external adaptation
• Structured control or exploratory movement

Step 2 — Question Weight Mapping
Question 1 (Weight 3) – Dominant Thought Content
Concrete present focus → add 3 to S
Future trajectory, symbolism, abstraction → add 3 to N
Structural analysis, system refinement → add 3 to T
Value rumination, moral reflection → add 3 to F
Inner conceptual refinement → add 1 to I
Outer engagement with events → add 1 to E
Question 2 (Weight 2) – Field of Interest
Systems, engineering, optimization → add 2 to T
Symbolism, psychology, meaning → add 2 to N (or F if value-centered)
Physical mastery, real-world engagement → add 2 to S
Ethics, harmony, authenticity motives → add 2 to F
Question 3 (Weight 2) – Dark Impulse
Compulsion for control over reality → add 2 to inferior Se or Te indicator
Catastrophic imagination, unseen threats → add 2 to inferior N indicator
Emotional volatility or suppressed sentiment → add 2 to inferior F indicator
Fear of emotional exposure or relational chaos → add 2 to inferior Fe indicator
Question 4 (Weight 3) – Moral Values
Objective fairness, consistency, impersonal justice → add 3 to T
Authenticity, loyalty, relational harmony → add 3 to F
Collective ethics or social alignment → add 1 to E
Private conviction or inner moral code → add 1 to I
Question 5 (Weight 2) – Daily Routine
Structured execution, task control → add 2 to Te
Exploratory idea wandering → add 2 to Ne or Ni
Sensory immersion, direct action → add 2 to Se
Stability, repetition, internal referencing → add 2 to Si
Question 6 (Weight 3) – Problem Solving
Stepwise logical decomposition → add 3 to T
Value-aligned or people-centered resolution → add 3 to F
Pattern forecasting or long-range modeling → add 3 to N
Hands-on practical trial and adjustment → add 3 to S
External structuring → add 1 to E
Internal modeling → add 1 to I
Question 7 (Weight 1) – What You Like
Reinforce whichever function theme dominates the answer by adding 1 to that function.
Question 8 (Weight 1) – What You Hate
Often reflects inferior opposition. Add 1 to opposite of dominant candidate if consistent.

Step 3 — Maintain Tallies
Keep running totals for S, N, T, F.
Keep running totals for I and E.

Step 4 — Determine Dominant Function
The highest score among S, N, T, F becomes the dominant candidate.
Orientation is determined by whether I or E total is higher.
If tied, use Question 1 and Question 6 as tie-breakers.

Step 5 — Determine Auxiliary
Auxiliary must:
• Oppose rationality category (Judging vs Perceiving)
• Share same orientation as dominant
Example:
If dominant is Ni (introverted perceiving), auxiliary must be extraverted judging (Te or Fe). Choose whichever T or F score is higher.

Step 6 — Determine Inferior
Inferior is the direct opposite of dominant function:
Ni ↔ Se
Ne ↔ Si
Ti ↔ Fe
Te ↔ Fi
Fi ↔ Te
Fe ↔ Ti
Si ↔ Ne
Se ↔ Ni
Confirm using Question 3 dark impulse indicators.

Step 7 — Conflict Resolution
If S and N difference is 2 points or less, re-evaluate using only Question 1 and Question 6.
If T and F difference is 2 points or less, Question 4 overrides.
If I and E difference is 1 point or less, Question 5 determines orientation.

Step 8 — Reliability Check
Classification is unstable if:
• Top two functions differ by 2 points or less
• Orientation difference is 1 point or less
• Question 1 and Question 6 contradict final result

If unstable, result should be marked Tentative rather than definitive.
