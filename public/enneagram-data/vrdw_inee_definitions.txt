- Phase: A unique name given to any part of life which uniquely isolates itself for lifestyle.
	- Map: A unique name given to the particular location subject was living at the time of phase.
	- Submaps: Part of maps, or places where the subject had to be at in a routine.
		- Submap1: A name given to the particular submap, more could be added with different name.
			- Slice: Time slice from whole phase timeline when the submap becomes applicable.
			- Frequency: Frequency in which the subplace had to be visited.
	- Info: Basic information about subject for relating the phase to maturity level.
		- Age range: Range of age which the phase covers.
		- Occupation: Occupation of the subject at the time which phase covers up.
		- Illness: Any special illness that was for a chronic period of time.
	- Lifestyle: Captures various lifestyle aspects of the user during runtime of phase.
		- Routine: General daily routine that subject followed during the phase.
		- Facilities: Exceptional facilities that the subject had at the time of phase.
		- Scarcities: Exceptional scarcities that the subject had at the time of phase.
	- Environment: General social atmosphere around the subject, including living conditions.
		- Locality: Tells what kind of locality was around subject, in one word or phrase.
		- People: Information what kind of interaction was occurring between subject and people regularly involved in phases.
			- Guardian relation: Relationship with whoever was the guardian of the subject at the time of phase.
			- Sibling relation: Relationship with biological siblings or cousins at the time.
			- Friends relation: Relationship with friends in general which the subject had at the time.
			- Mentor relation: Relationship with mentors in general at the time of phase (if any).
		- Society: Information about how society or general people around worked.
			- Elements: What the society consisted of, and who were the people involved.
			- Societal values: Values that the group of people around that formed society possessed.
	- Moments: Very special and notable moments that subject might remember about from the phase, whether experience was positive or negative.
		- Situation: Situation that occurred to make the moment notable, positively or negatively.
		- Conclusion: Conclusion that the situation being discussed reached.
